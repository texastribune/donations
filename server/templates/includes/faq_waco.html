Frequently Asked Questions

  • Who is behind this?

    The Texas Tribune, a nonprofit, nonpartisan statewide news organization, is evolving to bring more local news to communities like Waco as a means of engaging more Texans.

  • Who is supporting a new local newsroom in Waco?

    The Texas Tribune is leading this work with support from The Waco Foundation and the Cooper Foundation, along with local philanthropists. We hope to be in front of the Rapoport Foundation this fall. We continue to seek additional support for this work.

  • Why Waco?

    Texas is growing fast and Texans’ information needs are growing, too.

    A 2021 study on Waco conducted by a leading venture philanthropy organization found that there was an overwhelming need and want for more community news in Waco.

    The Texas Tribune is in a unique position to be part of the solution to solving for some of the news and information gaps experienced.

  • What will the newsroom report on?

    The newsroom will provide online free news and information on things like public education, economic development, health care and other topics that matter most to Wacoans.

    Coverage will also center on how to give and receive help, staying with stories to help residents understand what is happening and why, etc.

    Stories will supplement good work done by Waco’s current local media, and will be free to read and free to republish.

  • When will the newsroom launch?

    We expect to launch in Q4 of 2024 with community events and an email newsletter. We hope to be fully operational in Q1.
